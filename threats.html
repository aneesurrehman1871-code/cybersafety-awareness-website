1. Phishing Attacks

What is it? Phishing is a fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and credit card details by pretending to be a trustworthy source.

How it works: Attackers send fake emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate organizations (like banks, schools, or social media platforms) asking you to click a link or provide personal information.

Example: An email claiming "Your account will be suspended unless you verify your password immediately" with a suspicious link.

2. Malware

What is it? Malware (malicious software) includes viruses, trojans, spyware, and ransomware designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

How it spreads: Through infected email attachments, malicious websites, or downloading pirated software.

Effects: Can steal personal data, corrupt files, slow down systems, or lock you out of your own device.

3. Social Engineering

What is it? A manipulation technique that tricks people into revealing confidential information or performing actions that compromise security.

Example: Someone pretending to be tech support asking for your password, or a fake friend request from someone trying to gather information about you.

4. Weak Passwords

The Problem: Using simple passwords like "123456", "password", or your birthdate makes it easy for hackers to gain access to your accounts.

Common mistakes: Using the same password for multiple accounts, sharing passwords with friends, or writing them down in visible places.

5. Unsecured Wi-Fi Networks

The Risk: Public Wi-Fi networks (in cafes, libraries, airports) are often unsecured, allowing hackers to intercept your data.

What can be stolen: Login credentials, personal messages, banking information, and browsing history.

6. Identity Theft

What is it? When someone steals your personal information (name, ID number, photos) to impersonate you online or commit fraud.

Sources: Oversharing on social media, data breaches, or falling for scams.
