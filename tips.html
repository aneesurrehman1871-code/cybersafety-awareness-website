Password Security

  • Create strong passwords: Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols (e.g., P@ssw0rd!2024)
  • Use unique passwords: Never use the same password for multiple accounts
  • Change passwords regularly: Update your passwords every 3-6 months
  • Use a password manager: Tools like LastPass or Bitwarden can securely store your passwords
  • Never share passwords: Even with friends or family members

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

  • What is 2FA? An extra layer of security that requires a second form of verification (like a code sent to your phone) in addition to your password
  • Where to enable it: Enable 2FA on email accounts, social media, banking apps, and any service that offers it
  • Why it matters: Even if someone steals your password, they cannot access your account without the second verification

Email and Message Safety

  • Verify sender identity: Check email addresses carefully for suspicious characters or misspellings
  • Don't click unknown links: Hover over links to preview the URL before clicking
  • Avoid downloading attachments: From unknown senders, as they may contain malware
  • Be skeptical of urgent requests: Legitimate organizations rarely ask for sensitive information via email

Social Media Privacy

  • Review privacy settings: Set your profile to private and control who can see your posts
  • Limit personal information: Avoid sharing your phone number, address, school name, or daily schedule publicly
  • Be careful with friend requests: Only accept requests from people you know in real life
  • Think before posting: Once something is online, it can be difficult to remove completely

Safe Browsing Habits

  • Look for HTTPS: Secure websites have "https://" in the URL and a padlock icon in the address bar
  • Avoid suspicious websites: Don't visit sites that offer pirated content or seem too good to be true
  • Keep software updated: Regularly update your browser, operating system, and antivirus software
  • Use antivirus protection: Install reputable antivirus software and run regular scans

Public Wi-Fi Safety

  • Avoid sensitive transactions: Don't access banking or enter passwords on public Wi-Fi
  • Use a VPN: A Virtual Private Network encrypts your internet connection
  • Turn off auto-connect: Disable automatic Wi-Fi connections on your devices
  • Use mobile data: When possible, use your cellular data for sensitive activities

Device Security

  • Lock your devices: Use PIN, password, fingerprint, or face recognition
  • Enable remote wipe: Set up the ability to erase data if your device is lost or stolen
  • Be careful with USB drives: Unknown USB devices may contain malware
  • Log out after use: Always log out of accounts when using shared or public computers

What to Do If You're a Victim

  • Change passwords immediately: For all affected accounts
  • Report to authorities: Contact your school, parents, or local cyber crime cell
  • Monitor accounts: Check for unauthorized activity regularly
  • Learn from the experience: Understand what went wrong and how to prevent it in the future
